About the Pomodoro Technique

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s. It uses a timer to break work into intervals, traditionally 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks.

During each Pomodoro (the 25-minute work interval or whatever you decide!), you focus solely on a single task, aiming to complete it by the end of the Pomodoro. After each Pomodoro, take a short break to rest and recharge.

By breaking work into manageable intervals and incorporating regular breaks, the Pomodoro Technique can help improve focus, productivity, and overall work efficiency.
